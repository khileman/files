COVID Infection Rates and Masking Report

2023-03-27

1. Summary of Findings

The data examined the relationship between masking rates and infection rates of COVID 19. The mask usage data comes from a survey conducted by the CDC in 2020 and showed, on average, 50% wore masks always with 8% of the population admitting to never wearing a mask. Vaccination rates vary across counties with the highest rate of vaccination being 95% of people reported fully vaccinated with the lowest vaccination rate being 11.3%. At the peak of the pandemic, there were 242604 cases per 100,000 people and 1480 deaths per 100,00 people.

2. Data and Methodology

The data measures COVID infection rates using both state and local health department reporting. The data began being compiled at the start of the pandemic, with the first case tracked on January 21, 2020. The data is organized by U.S. county. This data is combined with Centers for Disease Control data compiled via survey in 2020 regarding mask usage.
